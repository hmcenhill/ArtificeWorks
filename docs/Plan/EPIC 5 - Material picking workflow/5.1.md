## BOM and component inventory model

**Labels:** domain, backend

## Summary

Give `Product` a flat bill of materials and introduce an on-hand inventory for the component materials a BOM draws from. Seed the shared-platform catalog so the "70% shared BOM" pitch is literally true in the data. No messaging yet — this is the domain and data foundation the picking consumer (5.2) reserves against.

## Why

Everything in this epic reserves *something* against *something*. Before a consumer can pick materials, the domain has to know what a product is made of and what stock exists. Getting the model right once — a flat BOM plus a decrement-able on-hand quantity — is what lets 5.3 layer concurrency and all-or-nothing reservation on top without reshaping the schema.

- [x] Flat BOM on `Product`: a collection of `BomLine` (component + quantity-per-unit)
- [x] EF mapping for both, plus a migration

## Acceptance Criteria

- [x] Products carry a flat BOM; the Custodian, Delver, and Courier each resolve to a concrete list of components and quantities
- [x] Components carry an on-hand quantity that seed data populates
- [x] The three product lines share the majority of their components in the seeded data (the 70%-shared claim is verifiable, not aspirational — Epic 11's dashboard will visualize exactly this)
- [x] Migration applies cleanly to a fresh database

## Decisions (to confirm at story start)

- **`Component` is a new entity, distinct from `Product`.** A `Product` is a finished automaton (Custodian/Delver/Courier); a `Component` is an input material (chassis, power core, …). They are deliberately *not* unified yet — Epic 13 introduces multi-level BOMs where a component can itself be a manufactured assembly, and that's the right time to converge them. Flat-BOM-first keeps this story honest to the pipeline-first principle.
- **On-hand inventory lives on `Component` as a simple quantity**, not a separate warehouse/location aggregate. Reservation in 5.3 decrements it. No reserved-vs-available split yet — a plain on-hand count that reservation draws down is enough for the flat slice and keeps the concurrency story (5.3) about one number.
- **BOM is `IReadOnlyList<BomLine>` on `Product`**, each line a `(Component, uint QtyPerUnit)`. Effective demand for a work order = `QtyPerUnit × OrderItemQty`.
- **Naming:** `Component` for the catalog item, `BomLine` for a BOM entry. Flag if `Material`/`Part` reads better in the domain language before it's baked into a migration.
- **Seed location:** a code-based seeder (idempotent, runs at startup or via the existing migration/seed path) rather than raw SQL in a migration, so the shared-BOM intent stays readable. Confirm against how (if at all) products are seeded today.

## Notes

The seeded overlap is not just flavor — Epic 11's dashboard renders BOM commonality across the product lines, and Epic 13's "shared-platform payoff" makes it queryable. Seed it as if that's already true: a shared chassis, power core, and control stack across all three lines, with locomotion / tool-hands / sensors varying by trade. Write the seed so the shared percentage is easy to eyeball.
